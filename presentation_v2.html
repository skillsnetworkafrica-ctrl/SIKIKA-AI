A QUESTION FOR THE JUDGES

Look at this judging panel.
Why isn't there a single person with a disability
sitting among you?

It's not because people with disabilities can't judge — they absolutely can. It's because making this event accessible was never prioritized. A sign language interpreter costs $25–$60/hour. Braille materials take weeks to prepare. Live captions? Not even considered.

Every day, deaf and hard-of-hearing students sit in lecture halls hearing nothing. Visually impaired students sit in front of diagrams they cannot see. Two groups. One shared problem: the system was never built for them.

That is the problem.
And that is exactly what we solve.
