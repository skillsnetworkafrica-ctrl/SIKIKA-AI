## SLIDE 1 — The Challenge (Iranzi, 60s)

**[Walk to center stage. Pause. Look at the judges. Slowly scan the room.]**

> "Before I start, I'd love to ask the judges something."

**[Pause 2 seconds.]**

> "How many people with disabilities are represented in this room today?"

**[Let the silence land. Don't rush.]**

> "Chances are, very few. And that's not because people with disabilities lack talent or judgment — it's because making this event accessible would require a sign language interpreter at 25 to 60 dollars an hour, braille materials that take weeks to prepare, live captions. Costs that are too high, too complex, or simply overlooked."

> "Now imagine a university lecture hall. Same barriers. Multiplied by millions of students. Every single day."

> "My name is Iranzi Innocent. This is Team CAMPUS_IQ. And this — is SIKIKA."

**[Click to Slide 2.]**

**KEY PHRASE TO MEMORIZE:** *"How many people with disabilities are represented in this room today?"*

## SLIDE 2 — The Numbers (Iranzi, 75s)

> "Let me give you the numbers. Not estimates — sourced facts."

**[Point to each row on screen as you speak.]**

> "430 million people worldwide have disabling hearing loss. That's the World Health Organization, 2021."

> "2.2 billion people have vision impairment. WHO, 2019."

> "In Kenya alone — the country we're standing in — there are approximately 200 certified sign language interpreters. For 54 million people. That's one interpreter for every 270,000 citizens. Kenya National Commission on Human Rights, 2022."

> "For the universities that can afford an interpreter, it costs 50 to 300 thousand dollars per year."

> "And even when an interpreter IS present, research shows students still lose 20 to 30 percent of lecture content. That's Marschark and colleagues, published 2015."

> "And the number of real-time accessibility tools designed for African universities? Zero."

**[Pause. Let "zero" hang.]**

> "Zero. Until today."

## SLIDE 4 — The Solution (Deogracia, 60s)

> "SIKIKA — the Swahili word for 'to be heard.' That's what we built."

> "Here's how it works. Step one: the lecturer speaks into any browser. No app download. No special hardware. Just a browser and a microphone."

> "Step two: SIKIKA's AI engine processes that speech in real time — transcribing, analyzing, and converting."

> "Step three: every student in the room receives the lecture in their preferred format. Simultaneously. Instantly."

> "Step four: deaf students see live captions with technical terms highlighted. Blind students hear audio descriptions. Hard-of-hearing students get sign language avatars."

> "Four pillars make this possible: it's 100% browser-based, it costs zero dollars per session, latency is under one second, and it works on any device — phone, tablet, laptop."

**[Click to Slide 5.]**

**KEY PHRASE TO MEMORIZE:** *"SIKIKA — the Swahili word for 'to be heard.'"*

## SLIDE 5 — Three Modes (Deogracia, 45s)

> "Let me walk you through the three accessibility modes."

> "Caption Mode — for deaf and hard-of-hearing students. Live transcription appears on screen with smart term highlighting. Technical vocabulary is flagged automatically so students don't miss critical concepts."

> "Audio Mode — for blind and visually impaired students. The transcript is converted to natural speech in real time. Students can adjust speed, pause, and replay."

> "Sign Mode — for students who use sign language as their first language. Text is converted to animated sign language, bridging the gap when no human interpreter is available."

> "Plus: personal accessibility sessions — students can customize font size, contrast, speech rate, and more. And every session can be replayed later for revision."

**[Click to Slide 6.]**

## Delivery Notes

### Pacing
- **Slide 1**: Slow, dramatic. The opening question needs silence after it.
- **Slides 2-3**: Authoritative, data-driven. Each stat gets its own beat.
- **Slides 4-5**: Energetic, building momentum. This is the "here's what we built" section.
- **Slide 6**: Wonder and pride. This is the "wow" moment.
- **Slide 7**: Confident, calm. Let the technology speak for itself.
- **Slide 8**: Sharp, business-like. Numbers, numbers, numbers.
- **Slide 9**: Visionary but grounded. Don't oversell the future — anchor in today.
- **Slide 10**: Quiet power. Don't shout. Let the words do the work.
