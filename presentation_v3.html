SECTION 1 — THE PROBLEM

Education was never built for
students with disabilities.

Every day, deaf and hard-of-hearing students sit in lecture halls hearing nothing. Visually impaired students sit in front of diagrams they cannot see. The information is there — they simply cannot access it in real time.

✗ Who is affected

  • 430 million people with disabling hearing loss globally
  • 2.2 billion people with vision impairment globally
  • University students in Africa with no interpreter access
  • 15% of the world population lives with some form of disability

ⓘ Where it happens

  • University lecture halls, tutorials, and labs
  • In-person learning environments with zero real-time support
  • Especially severe in Sub-Saharan Africa where interpreter supply is critically low
  • Kenya: ~200 certified interpreters for 54 million people

WHO World Report on Hearing 2021 · WHO World Vision Report 2019 · KNCHR 2022 · WHO Disability Report 2023

THE PROBLEM — EVIDENCE

The numbers are undeniable.

Certified sign language interpreters in ALL of Kenya ~200 KNCHR 2022; WASLI
That means one interpreter for every 270,000 Kenyans 54M population / ~200 interpreters
Cost of one interpreter per university per year $50,000 – $300,000 $25–$60/hr × 5hrs/day × 200 days
Content LOST even with a human interpreter 20–30% Marschark et al. 2015; Napier 2014

Why it matters: You cannot hire enough humans. The supply does not exist. These students are not failing because they lack ability — they are failing because the system was never designed for them.

Sources: WHO 2021, KNCHR 2022, WASLI, Marschark et al. 2015, Napier 2014
